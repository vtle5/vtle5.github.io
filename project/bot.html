Lets make a bot

I had just finished prototyping my multiplayer project and as much as I wanted to continue working on it, I wanted to do something else. Eventually I figured out a new project to work on, making a Discord bot! Discord is a communication platform that me and my friends frequently use. Having a bot would be fun and useful to have around, so I began creating one. I had decided to use discord.js as there was more documentation for it when compared to its counterpart discord.py. Soon enough, the bot went online and was able to respond to a ping message.

Hello World!

I began adding further functionality to the bot and cleaned up the inner workings. I found myself spending hours in the discord.js documentation figuring out its capabilities and limits. I soon added a command structure so that users can use the bot through a command prefix, such as "-help". This would make further testing easier. The bot soon was able to add reactions to messages, respond to commands and reply to users if they had said anything specific. I later gave the bot the ability to display a custom status and to perform commands such as flipping a coin and rolling numbers. For server moderation, the bot was able to move users to different voice channels and to temporarily ban users for a short amount of time.

Music, Cats and API

The bot was fairly basic and I wanted to add even more functionality, so I looked making the bot capable of playing music into a voice channel. This was a complex task and required several additional packages to be installed. After a lot of debugging, the bot was finally able to play music when given a Youtube link. As a final feature, I wanted to have the bot access an API. I decided on using simple APIs where the bot would only need to retrieve basic information and display it. This process was easier than the music, so the bot soon was able to provide fun facts and cat pictures on demand.
